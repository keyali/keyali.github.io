Hi there! I'm Siting

I am a researcher (maybe) bridging the gap between dreams and real life (please give me a well-paid job...). Currently, I am pursuing my Master's in Social and Cultural Anthropology at KU Leuven , with an Erasmus exchange at Lund University.

From Data to the Field

My academic path is a bit unconventional. I started with a Bachelor's in Economics from Zhejiang University. But I realized I wanted to understand the human stories behind data and models. This curiosity led me to anthropology, where I could explore the complexities of culture, identity, and human relationships. One pivotal moment was during a research project on rural development, where I saw firsthand how numbers often fail to capture the lived realities of people. This realization inspired me to shift my focus from abstract models to the tangible, nuanced world of ethnography.

What I Do Now

Today, my research focuses on human-animal relations, specifically how Kazakh herders and their horses navigate modernization and state policies in the Sino-Kazakh borderlands. Instead of just crunching numbers, I now conduct immersive ethnographic fieldwork—navigating winter pastures, learning basic Kazakh greetings, and listening to the stories of the herders. One of the most memorable moments was riding alongside herders during a migration, feeling the biting cold and witnessing the deep bond between them and their horses. My work aims to shed light on how traditional practices adapt and persist in the face of modern challenges.

A Few Fun Facts

  • Toolkit: I am equally comfortable running STATA models (not at all) and writing reflexive field notes (actually complaining). I also dabble in qualitative data analysis tools and enjoy experimenting with creative ways to present research findings.
  • Languages: I speak Mandarin and English fluently, am learning French (A2), and know just enough Kazakh to say hello and thank you. I believe language is a window into culture, and I enjoy the challenge of picking up new phrases wherever I go.
  • Passions: I have a soft spot for horses, and I am fascinated by K-pop (only girl groups). My favorite groups inspire me with their artistry and dedication, and I often find parallels between their stories and the resilience of the communities I study.
  • Hobbies: I like singing, exploring new places on a budget, and indulging in a good book with a cup of tea. I also enjoy trying local cuisines and believe food is one of the best ways to connect with people and their cultures.
  • Beyond the Pasture: I've also researched abortion narratives in Belgium and worked in HR focusing on international labor mobility. These experiences have broadened my perspective and deepened my understanding of global interconnectedness.
