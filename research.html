Research Projects

My academic interests revolve around multi-species ethnography, pastoralism, and quantitative-qualitative mixed methods.

Current Research

Human-Horse Relations and Pastoral Change among Kazakh Herders in Ili

Master's Thesis Research, KU Leuven | Nov. 2024 - Present

  • Conducted 3-month immersive ethnographic fieldwork in the Sino-Kazakh borderlands.
  • Applied multi-sited ethnography across pastures, markets, and administrative settings from domestic winter pastures (Dongwozi) to state-managed horse ranches.
  • Investigated human-horse relations beyond livestock value, using the horse economy as a lens to understand cultural resilience.

Previous Projects

Ethnographic Study on Abortion Experiences

Course-based Project, KU Leuven | Sep. 2024 - Jan. 2025

  • Conducted in-depth interviews and participant observation to explore abortion experiences, reproductive autonomy, and the social shaping of narratives in Belgium.
  • Engaged with a local reproductive healthcare clinic to investigate structural barriers to abortion accessibility.

Bride Price and Female Education: A Quantitative Study in China

Bachelor's Thesis, Zhejiang University | Sep. 2023 - May. 2024

  • Collected and processed nearly 30,000 bride-price dispute cases from the China Judgments Online database using Python.
  • Applied panel data econometric models to analyze the impact of women's average years of education on bride-price levels.
